Basic Chess Rules


There are 16 pieces for each player. There are 6 different pieces that do different things. There is the bishop, knight rook, king, queen and pawn. The Bishop only moves diagonally, but can move as many squares and it wants, shown below.

The pawn can move two squares foward if it is its first move. Otherwise it only moves 1 square fowards, and takes pieces diagonally, shown below.

The knight goes in a L shape. It goes 3 blocks straight, and one block another direction. Shown below.

The rook goes in straight lines, horizonally and verically.

The queen moves in all directions, diagonally, horizonally and vertically, and as far as she wants. This makes her the most power piece in chess.

The king can move in any direction like the queen, but can only move 1 square. The king can also be check and checkmated. How you check the king is by moving a piece so it could take the king. Checkmate is where you check the king, but the king can move no where. When you checkmate you win. You must note that if you are not checking the king, and the king and his pieces can not move, it is a draw.

Basic Opening Strategys

Rule one of the early game. Try to control the center. Basically what I mean by that is put the pawns in the center.

Rule two of the early game. Delevop your pieces. What I mean by this is to move your pieces out of their original position. Note, don't try to move your rook out in the first few moves It's a Bad Idea

Rule three of the early game. Get your king to safety. Castle or something but just get your king to safety. The position of the king originally is not the safest and a good opponent will exploit it. Of course you should not castle if you were castling right into danger.

Rule four of early game. This rule is mostly situational but don't move your queen out in the early game. This is because you could move your queen out, but then they would move their knight out, going to take your queen, so you will have to move back. Then they will get a advantage is delevoping your pieces.
